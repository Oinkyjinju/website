Quick view of managing In-country Designers

QA process

When the original title treatment is given to create a localized design, there are multiple ways of providing in-country designers instructions. One of the methods i use to deliver culturally relevant localized title is to find the synopsis and offer a different style of title treatment as needed based on in-country production culture.

Title Seventeen is an excellent example of proactively providing different styles of title treatment to the client. The Korean film industry is huge because it requires special care of pre-productions that is highly culturally relevant and aesthetically pleasing and must have a clear message within the design.
First, I allowed the in-country designer to create a localized title with an English serif style. The original is designed and suggested two different styles of in-market titles with an example of an image that is more culturally relevant with the synopsis to the client. Once the suggestion is approved, I send a new request to our in-country designer to create a new TT style with more detailed instructions.

We made two additional designs that both can work for the movie Seventeen, and once we send them out to the client, they chose version 2 as the final version.
